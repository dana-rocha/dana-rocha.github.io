Education

My informatics training was cross-disciplinary - combining Biology, Computer Science, and Statistics. During my Master's program, I learned how to code in Perl and Python through a functional programming paradigm. I gained experience with unit testing, file manipulation, program design, and implementation.

My data science training was focused on data wrangling, data visualization, and regression applied to biological data and social science data. I also have experience using machine learning techniques such as Clustering, Principal Component Analysis, Support Vector Machines, and Neural Networks. R is my language of choice when it comes to data science. I'm a big fan of tidyverse - particularly the ggplot2 package.
